1Executive Summary

What this should become — and why it matters

Today's What-If module answers "what does this one change do?". The next version should answer the questions that actually keep a portfolio landlord awake: should I buy, refinance, or pay down debt — and which path gets me to financial independence fastest, at the lowest risk? That is the leap from a calculator to a Portfolio Intelligence Platform.

🧭

The product

A decision-support cockpit: model any strategy across 10–20 years, compare strategies side-by-side, set goals and let the system find the pathways, and surface risk before it bites.

⚖️

Why it matters

A single £80k buy-vs-refinance decision moves lifetime net worth by six figures. Investors currently make these calls in spreadsheets that ignore tax, voids, and rate shocks. This removes the guesswork.

🎯

The differentiator

Not accounting software and not a yield calculator — a forward-looking engine that ranks strategies by cashflow, ROI, risk, equity and time-to-target, grounded in the user's real assets.

The live portfolio is under-leveraged (24% LTV) and highly concentrated (100% Central Scotland flats). That is precisely the situation where a what-if engine earns its keep: there is dry powder to deploy, a clear £5k/10-property goal, and a real question of leverage vs. cash-stacking. The platform's first job is to show, in pounds, which route wins — and what it would take to break.

✅ Bottom line: the engine and data model are already 60% of the way there. The missing 40% — comparison UI, goals, risk, and richer financial modelling — is what turns a working feature into a product investors would pay for.

Strengths, weaknesses & the gap

💪 Strengths

  • Pure, testable engine — buildProjection can be memoised, run client-side, or in a web worker without DB coupling.
  • Clean separation — calculations live server-side as the single source of truth; the engine is decoupled from persistence.
  • Extensible schema — scenario_events already stores arbitrary parameters_json; new event types need no migration of shape.
  • Deterministic — same inputs → same output, ideal for caching, diffing and comparison.

⚠️ Weaknesses & missing capability

  • Over-simplified finance — hardcoded 3% value growth and 1% linear debt decay (not real amortisation); breaks under downturns or high rates.
  • No tax or transaction costs — a £66k Scottish buy silently ignores ~£5,280 of ADS plus legal/survey fees.
  • One scenario at a time — no way to see Base vs. Growth vs. Deleverage together.
  • Opaque events — parameters shown as truncated raw JSON; no per-property drill-down in results.
  • Manual everything — must click "Run" after each edit; no goals, no risk flags, no insights.
🧱Strategic read: the foundation is strong precisely where it is hardest to retrofit (a clean, pure engine and a flexible event schema). The gaps are in the layers above it — modelling fidelity, comparison, goals and risk — which can be added incrementally without rewriting the core.

Tax modelling — and why Scotland matters here

This portfolio is in Scotland, so the platform must use LBTT + the Additional Dwelling Supplement (ADS), currently 8% — not English SDLT. Ignoring it materially overstates every acquisition's return.

Worked example — the £66k Comely Place cash buy

Purchase price£66,000
LBTT (residential bands, <£145k)£0
ADS @ 8% on full price£5,280
Legal + survey (est.)£1,500
True cash-in (vs. £66,000 modelled)£72,780

Today's engine would show this buy as £6,780 cheaper than reality — enough to flip a "good deal" verdict. Transaction-cost accuracy is a P0.

The post-tax cashflow layer

  • Personal name: Section 24 — mortgage interest is no longer deductible; relief is a 20% tax credit. High-rate landlords pay tax on turnover, not profit.
  • Ltd / SPV: corporation tax on profit, interest fully deductible; model director's-loan extraction.
  • On sale: CGT at residential-property rates above the annual exemption (personal), or corp tax on gain (Ltd).
  • Output: every scenario gains a post-tax net-cashflow series alongside the pre-tax one — the number that actually funds FI.
🧾 For this low-leverage portfolio S24 bites little today, but it becomes decisive the moment leverage is added — exactly the buy-vs-cash question on the table.

Assumptions become first-class inputs

The hardcoded 3% growth and 1% decay move into a per-scenario Assumptions panel: regional capital growth, rent inflation (CPI-linked), maintenance reserve %, void allowance, mortgage rate outlook, and tax structure. Every projection becomes reproducible and defensible — and stress tests are just the same model with shocked assumptions.

The diff view — the real insight

🤖 Path to 10 vs. 4 Properties: +£673k projected equity and it is the only route to £5,000/mo — but it spends ~14 months with liquidity under one month's outgoings and peaks at 46% LTV. The premium for reaching the goal is concentration of risk into the 2027–2030 build phase.

How the comparison engine works

Normalise

Each scenario's stored results_json is reduced to a common metric vector (end equity, avg/terminal net CF, peak LTV, min-DSCR, liquidity trough, time-to-target).

Rank & flag

Per-dimension ranking with best-in-column highlighting; a weighted composite score (user-tunable weights) gives an overall recommendation.

Explain

Pairwise diffs translated into plain-English trade-offs, so the output reads like an analyst's memo, not a grid of numbers.

AI-style insights — what the analyst would say

🤖Concentration alert. 100% of equity sits in three Central Scotland flats. Diversifying the next 2–3 acquisitions by region/type would lift the diversification score from 22 → ~55 with negligible yield sacrifice.
🤖Under-utilised capacity. At 24% LTV you have ~£90k of prudent borrowing headroom. Deployed via Pathway B it brings the £5k/mo goal forward by ~4 years.

8Technical Architecture

Build on the pure engine, not around it

The architecture deliberately preserves the existing crown jewel — a pure, deterministic buildProjection() — and layers new capability as composable modules above it. Nothing here requires a rewrite; it is additive.

Layered engine design

Calculation engine

The atomic, side-effect-free maths: amortisation schedules, yield/ROI/LTV/DSCR, LBTT+ADS, S24 & CGT. Lives server-side in services/calculations.ts as the single source of truth; mirrored read-only client-side for live form preview.

New: amortise(), taxEngine(), transactionCosts() as pure helpers.

Projection engine

The month-by-month loop — buildProjection(initialState, events, config) in services/scenarioEngine.ts. Stays pure. Gains a richer MonthSnapshot (post-tax CF, DSCR, liquidity, per-property breakdown) and an assumptions object replacing hardcoded constants.

Scenario engine

Orchestration: runScenario() hydrates state from the DB, applies events, persists scenario_results. Extended with a comparison service (reduce N results → metric vectors) and a goal solver (search event sequences to hit a target).

Intelligence engine

Reads projections + portfolio facts to compute risk/health/liquidity/opportunity scores and generate insight strings. Pure functions over results — cacheable and testable like everything else.

Event system & data model

The current schema already carries the design: scenario_events stores event_type, property_id, date, sort_order and free-form parameters_json — so new event types need no shape migration. Extensions:

Table / objectChangePurpose
scenario_events.event_typeAdd refinance (implement), capital_injection, sale_with_costs, maintenance_reserve to the CHECKNew modelling capability via the existing numbered-migration pattern.
scenarios.assumptions_jsonNew columnPer-scenario growth, inflation, void %, reserve %, tax structure — replaces hardcoded 3%/1%.
scenario_results.results_jsonRicher snapshot schemaPost-tax CF, DSCR, liquidity, per-property arrays for drill-down & comparison.
goals + goal_pathwaysNew tablesPersist targets, constraints and generated/ranked pathways.
Event registryTyped handler map keyed by event_typeEach event = a pure (state, params) ⇒ state reducer; adding a type is a registry entry + test.

Performance & extensibility

⚡

Performance

Engine is pure ⇒ memoise by (stateHash, eventsHash, assumptionsHash). Run comparison fan-out and goal-solver iterations in a web worker to keep the UI responsive; SQLite stays synchronous and fast for the single-user model.

🔁

Caching & diffing

Determinism enables result caching, instant re-display, and cheap pairwise diffs for the comparison view — only re-run scenarios whose inputs changed.

🧩

Extensibility

New event types, new score formulas and new goal templates are all plug-in registry entries with unit tests — no engine surgery. Calculations stay server-authoritative per the project's core pattern.

🏗️ Architectural principle: keep the projection engine pure and deterministic; push all I/O, persistence and orchestration to the edges. Every new feature in this document is expressible as pure functions over the existing engine — which is why the roadmap is incremental, not a rewrite.

UK tax rates and thresholds (LBTT/ADS, S24, CGT, corporation tax) change at fiscal events; the platform should treat them as configurable, dated parameters rather than constants.

Glossary

  • LTV — Loan-to-Value: debt ÷ property value.
  • DSCR — Debt Service Coverage Ratio: net operating income ÷ debt payments. Lenders & the stress floor watch this.
  • LBTT — Land & Buildings Transaction Tax (Scotland's stamp duty).
  • ADS — Additional Dwelling Supplement: surcharge on second+ homes (currently 8%).
  • Section 24 (S24) — restricts mortgage-interest relief for personally-held BTL to a 20% credit.
  • CGT — Capital Gains Tax on disposal gains.
  • BRRR — Buy, Refurbish, Refinance, Rent: recycle deposits via equity release.
  • Cash-on-cash — annual net cashflow ÷ actual cash invested.
  • Void — period a property is untenanted (no rent).
  • FI — Financial Independence: passive income covers living costs.
